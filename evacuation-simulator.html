▶  Methodology & Model Documentation

1 Readiness Scoring Model

Destination readiness is a confidence-weighted average of seven factor scores. Two factors are gatekeepers (weight 2×) — non-substitutable prerequisites under IHL: a physically dangerous or legally inaccessible destination cannot be made viable by strong food supply or willing hosts.

readiness = (Σ w_i × score_i) / Σ w_i w = 2 (gatekeeper) | w = 1 (standard) If any gatekeeper BLOCKED: readiness = min(readiness, 0.20)
StatusScoreNotes
Operational1.0 × confFully functional; confidence-scaled

2 Uncertainty Model

Uncertainty operates at two levels. The Field Uncertainty slider scales all factor confidences multiplicatively:

effectiveConf = baseConf × (1 − fieldUncertainty)

Unknown status encodes a key epistemic distinction: a known unknown (high-confidence "I don't know") is less dangerous than an unknown unknown (low-confidence, unreliable assessment):

score_unknown = 0.30 × (0.5 + 0.5 × effectiveConf) conf=1.0 → score=0.30 (we know we don't know) conf=0.0 → score=0.15 (we don't know what we don't know)

This models the real-world asymmetry: a field team reporting "unassessed" provides actionable intelligence; a report produced under unreliable conditions does not.

3 Monte Carlo Outcome Simulation

For each (group, destination) pair, 100 Monte Carlo runs simulate outcome distributions under field uncertainty. Each run independently perturbs every factor status:

P(perturb) = 0.85 × (1 − effectiveConf) If perturbed: status shifts ±1 ordinal level operational ↔ partial ↔ blocked ↔ unknown

Success in a single run requires all three conjunctive conditions:

  • Readiness ≥ 40% (minimum viability threshold)
  • Destination capacity ≥ group size
  • No gatekeeper blocked (legal/security access maintained)

Reported success rate = proportion of runs meeting all three. Standard deviation quantifies outcome volatility — high SD indicates a fragile scenario.

4 Assignment Algorithm

Each evacuee group is assigned to the destination with the highest composite score:

composite = 0.40 × readiness + 0.30 × capacity_fit min(1, cap/size) + 0.20 × proximity 1 − dist/max_dist + 0.10 × vuln_match 1.0 if medical=op & needs, else 0.5

Weights reflect humanitarian priority ordering: protection quality → physical fit → operational burden → population needs. Capacity fit is capped at 1.0 to prevent over-rewarding oversized sites.

5 Seeded RNG & Synthetic Data

All values use the Park-Miller Linear Congruential Generator — no Math.random() anywhere:

s_{n+1} = (s_n × 16807) mod 2147483647 Period: 2³¹ − 2 ≈ 2.1 × 10⁹ Ref: Park & Miller (1988), CACM 31(10)

A fixed seed guarantees full reproducibility — any peer reviewer can regenerate any scenario exactly from its seed number. Destination capacity uses an exponential distribution (λ=3, mapped [200–5000]) reflecting the realistic skew of humanitarian site sizes.

7 Assumptions & Limitations

Known modelling simplifications

  • Factor statuses treated as independent — real-world correlations (e.g. security and authority consent co-vary) are not modelled.
  • The 40% readiness threshold is an operational approximation; it should be calibrated against empirical data in applied use.
  • Status perturbation shifts exactly one ordinal level; real mis-assessments may skip levels.
  • Groups are assigned independently — no capacity contention is modelled between groups assigned to the same destination.
  • Distance is a proxy for operational burden; routing constraints (checkpoints, fuel, road access) are not captured.

Intended use & scope

  • This is a conceptual demonstration instrument, not an operational planning system. Outputs require validation against empirical field data before any real-world use.
  • Designed to illustrate uncertainty propagation through an evacuation chain and to support the argument that AI-assisted real-time field assessment improves outcome predictability.
  • MC N=100 is sufficient for directional insight; production use warrants N≥1000 for tight confidence intervals.
  • All simulation parameters live in the PARAMS constant at the top of the script — adjustable for sensitivity analysis without touching any other code.
